Children are central to our mission at Youth Crime Watch of Liberia, guiding all our child-friendly initiatives, including our eight Street Library Centers, as we actively embrace their role as the future.

Our Mission

Youth Crime Watch of Liberia (YCWL) is a national, non-governmental and nonprofit organization that exists to empower, improve, and transform the lives and livelihood of young people in our country.

Since our inception, our focus has been to re-educate our young population with the spirit of entrpreneurship and national growth. Over the years, with the progress of our peace and the stability of our country, we have expanded our original mission to add more focus on child development across all our programs and all counties in which we operate.

Our Thematic Areas

Youth Crime Watch of Liberia is a not-for-profit organization that is dynamic, community-focused, and dedicated to making a meaningful impact on the lives of individuals in our country. Since our establishment, we have been actively engaged in a wide range of activities designed to foster personal and community development. Our core mission is to create positive change, empower individuals, and contribute to the betterment of society. With those in mind, we have organized our activities into several thematic areas, each with sore programs that serve specific communities in all counties where we operate.

Child Protection

Liberia is a nation characterized by its youthful population, with a significant demographic imbalance. An astonishing 42 percent of the population is comprised of children under the age of 15, and a staggering 63 percent are under the age of 25. This demographic makeup presents Liberia with a formidable challenge: to safeguard its young citizens from harm while concurrently providing them with the space and opportunities to develop and lead the country toward a brighter future.

Crime Prevention and Peacebuilding

Under this thematic area, Youth Crime Watch of Liberia is dedicated to implementing strategies and initiatives that target the reduction of crime, the improvement of community safety, and the advancement of peace and conflict resolution in both urban and rural communities in Liberia. This encompasses a diverse array of activities and methodologies crafted to thwart criminal activities, alleviate violence, and foster the development of harmonious and resilient communities.

Economic Development & Livelihood

Liberia faces various challenges associated with livelihood and entrepreneurship, including high levels of poverty, limited access to finance, inadequate infrastructure, lack of skills and education, and insufficient institutional support. Several problems affect livelihoods and entrepreneurship in Liberia. Youth Crime Watch of Liberia Economic Development & Livelihood thematic area is a comprehensive area aimed at fostering sustainable economic growth, improving the livelihoods of individuals and communities, and reducing poverty.

Education & Literacy

While education in Liberia is theoretically free for students attending government schools, most of these institutions suffer from inadequate learning facilities. Consequently, many parents opt to send their children to private schools, which often come with a hefty price tag that is beyond the means of the average Liberian family. The 14-year civil war, followed by the 2014 Ebola outbreak that led to school closures, had a profound and enduring impact on the education system.

Gender & Women Empowerment

Gender discrimination and deeply ingrained social norms have disproportionate effects on males and females, subjecting them to distinct challenges. Specifically, females often find themselves at risk of child marriage, teenage pregnancy, child labor, limited access to quality education and healthcare, sexual abuse, exploitation, and various forms of violence. In contrast, males can also fall victim to issues such as sodomy, mental abuse, stigmatization, bullying, domestic violence, and sexual abuse.

Governance, Accountability, and Leadership (GAL)

In most communities in Liberia, especially rural areas, the populations have limited opportunities for civic engagement due to geographical isolation and lower levels of education, affecting their participation in key decision-making processes. Youth Crime Watch of Liberia's GAL thematic area is dedicated to conducting comprehensive research and delivering insightful analyses on critical democratic and governance matters.

Environment & Climate Change

Youth Crime Watch of Liberia (YCWL) is actively promoting sustainable and concerted efforts to combat climate change and raise awareness about its profound impact as the world undergoes urbanization and industrialization. Success Stories

Overtime, we affect many people's lives. While all stories are important, sometimes we encounter incredible people whose stories bring hope to all of us. The following are a few of those stories:

Princess Folley, 10

Prior to Princess attending the Street Library, her reading proficiency was poor. She arrived with an eager attitude to learn more than the few words she was able to read and the short words she was able to pronounce. After three months of attending the Street Library her reading level has gone from a one to a three! Princess now takes great pride in pronouncing six and eight-letter words.

Princess Johnson, 9

Unlike many children who are fortunate to start school early, Princess Johnson, 9, began her primary education at age 7. But because of her willingness to learn and the availability of sufficient learning materials and passionate volunteers to enhance her learning progress, Princess Johnson has showcased exceptional academic performance at her school and the Street Library.

Jeter Peters, 12

Like so many children in Liberia, Jeter had no prospect of attaining any sort of formal education. His parents moved him from one place to another, from one relative to another, hoping he will eventually gain access to education. Unfortunately, that did not work out for him. Having done some research, his sister enrolled him in our TVET program, and Jeter has become an incredible tailor.
